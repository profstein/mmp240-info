## Wireframes

<p>What are frames are important part of the web design process and they are a chance for us to think through the content and layout of our pages before we actually start writing HTML and CSS. The basic idea behind wire frames is to provide a visualization of how the page will look. Ideally wire frames don't take that much time so you can quickly visualize and also change them if need be.</p>
<p>Sometimes you will hear of a couple types of wire frames one type being low fidelity or lo-fi wire frames and another being high fidelity or hi-fi.</p>
<h3><strong>Lo-Fi vs Hi-Fi Wireframes</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Low-fidelity (Lo-Fi) wireframes</strong> are quick, simplified sketches of a web page or app layout. They focus on <strong>structure, hierarchy, and user flow</strong> ‚Äî not on visuals. Think of them as ‚Äúblueprints‚Äù showing where things go (like navigation, content blocks, buttons) without worrying about color, font, or detailed alignment. They don't have final text and sometime just use solid blocks to represent text. They‚Äôre fast to make and easy to change, which makes them ideal for early-stage planning, brainstorming, and feedback.</p>
<p><strong>High-fidelity (Hi-Fi) wireframes</strong>, on the other hand, are more detailed and often created digitally (e.g., in Figma).&nbsp;</p>
<p>Mockups are a realistic rendering of what the site will look like. They include <strong>typography, color, spacing, and often interactivity</strong> to simulate the real user experience. These are typically used later in the design process to refine visual design and prepare for development.</p>
